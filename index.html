PUP Quezon City Branch

Originating from petitions advocating the establishment of an educational institution catering to the youth in the National Government Center area, pushed by community groups and churches, and after talks with local leaders and government bodies, the PUP Quezon City Campus marks a clear win towards the struggle in people’s fight against ignorance. Officially laid out in a document on March 24, 1997, the goal was to "start a branch or extension of PUP at the National Government Center, providing quality education and affordable tuition to the urban poor in Quezon City, especially those from NGCHP." Since kicking off in 1997, PUP Quezon City has consistently produced solid graduates in education, IT, management, marketing, entrepreneurship, and office management technology, making a positive impact on the competitive global stage.

History

The PUP Quezon City Campus, formerly known as PUP Commonwealth Campus, encompasses a 1.9-hectare wooded area. It was officially transferred to the Polytechnic University of the Philippines (PUP) by the Housing and Urban Development Coordinating Council-National Government Center Housing Committee (HUDCC-NGCHC), facilitated through a Deed of Assignment signed in March 1997. This transfer aimed to establish a PUP branch at the National Government Center, focusing on delivering quality education and subsidized tuition to the urban poor communities of Quezon City.

Before this, the Sentro sa Ikauunlad ng Kasanayan at Hanapbuhay (SIKHAY) donated two halls, Rothlehner and Misereor Building, to PUP in February 1997. These buildings, named in honor of the funding donors, now serve various purposes on the campus.

PUP Quezon City commenced its operations in 1997, initially offering a Master in Educational Management under the Open University System. Over time, the campus expanded its offerings to include undergraduate programs such as Bachelor of Science in Information Technology, Bachelor in Business Teacher and Livelihood Education, Bachelor of Science in Business Administration, Bachelor of Science in Entrepreneurship, and Bachelor of Public Administration.

Additionally, the campus provides a Diploma in Office Management Technology with a specialization in Medical Office Management. True to PUP's commitment as the "Pamantasan ng Bayan," the campus offers graduate programs, including Master in Educational Management, Master in Information Technology, and Master in Public Administration through the Open University system.

Presently, PUP Quezon City Campus comprises four buildings, including a recently constructed Academic Building, an Ecumenical Chapel, the Winnie Castelo Welfare Complex, and a covered court. Designed as a "Green Campus," it mirrors the layout of the PUP Main Campus, providing a conducive learning environment for students from the northeastern part of Metro Manila, Rizal, and Bulacan.

The official recognition of PUP Quezon City Campus was solidified through the enactment of Republic Act 11347, known as the "PUP Quezon City Campus Act," signed into law on June 25, 2019. This legislative milestone, sponsored by Representative Winston "Winnie" T. Castelo and Senator Francis "Chiz" G. Escudero, underscores the university's dedication to democratizing educational opportunities for economically challenged students. Originating from community petitions and negotiations with local organizations, the campus stands as a testament to the success in the collective effort against ignorance and remains committed to providing quality education to urban poor communities in Quezon City and neighboring areas.

Vision

PUP: The National Polytechnic University

Mission Goals
Ensuring inclusive and equitable quality education and promoting lifelong learning opportunities through a re-engineered polytechnic university by committing to:
  • • Provide democratized access to educational opportunities for the holistic development of individuals with a global perspective
  • • Offer industry-oriented curricula that produce highly-skilled professionals with managerial and technical capabilities and a strong sense of public service for nation building
  • • Embed a culture of research and innovation
  • • Continuously develop faculty and employees with the highest level of professionalism
  • • Engage public and private institutions and other stakeholders for the attainment of social development goals
  • • Establish a strong presence and impact in the international academic community
 Transparency and Efficiency
  • Responsibly manage school-related activities that may contribute to the holistic development of all stakeholders in instruction, research, and extension;
System Performance
  • Utilize leadership, collaboration, and strategic partnership in institutional, national, and international activities;
Organizational Excellence
  • Taking accountability in delivering quality service through excellent performance of faculty members and administrative staff; and
Safety and Health
  • Promote health through worthwhile school activities involving faculty members, staffs, and students.
